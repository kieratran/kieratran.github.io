Hi, I am a 5th-year PhD student working with [Dr. Winnie Chu at Georgia Tech](https://glacier-geophys.eas.gatech.edu/) on exploring the applications of ice-penetrating radar in glaciology, specifically in ice-ocean interactions. My projects focus on creating a relationship between radar attenuation rates and basal melt rates, investigating the evolution of West Antarctic ice-shelf basal channels, and predicting ice-base topography using machine learning. 

Besides my main work, I have mentored students at the [NASA Student Airborne Research Program](https://espo.nasa.gov/sarp) on various topics, ranging from applications of interferometric synthetic aperture radar (InSAR) in assessing risks/damage from extreme natural events to satellite observations on vegetation growth/restoration. I am inspired by interacting with other scientists, teachers, and learners.
